0 · The problem & the play

Today: only 3 results on Google page 1 for "LendPeak" come from lendpeak.com — the rest are unrelated. For a brand term, that's leaking trust and clicks to noise. Target: ≥70% of page 1 owned or positive.

Why PR fixes this: a high-authority site that publishes an article about LendPeak will rank for the exact-match brand query "LendPeak". Place 4–5 of those and they push the unrelated results off page 1 — so page 1 becomes lendpeak.com pages + your PR articles + your Trustpilot/Crunchbase/LinkedIn profiles. Bonus: AI assistants (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) corroborate brands across 2–4 independent domains — the same PR placements are exactly what make LendPeak recommendable by AI. ([brand mentions = top AI-citation predictor](https://www.ekamoira.com/blog/ai-citations-llm-sources))

What "owns page 1" looks like: lendpeak.com (home, /funding/, working-capital), Trustpilot profile, Crunchbase/LinkedIn, + 4–5 PR articles titled around "LendPeak". That's comfortably 70%+.

4 · What to publish (YMYL rules — don't skip)

Finance is "Your Money or Your Life", so AI engines and Google weight trust heavily. Each article should:

  • Be brand-anchored: title/H1 includes "LendPeak" so it ranks for the brand query, and link to lendpeak.com.
  • Show real expertise + disclosure: named author/bio, "sponsored/partner" label where required, no hype claims ("guaranteed approval" etc.).
  • Be data-driven, not generic tips: SMB funding trends, cost-of-capital explainers, "MCA vs term loan", real LendPeak facts ($850M+ funded, 8+ years, all 50 states, 550+, 24-hour funding). These outperform fluff and get cited by AI.
  • Stay consistent: same name, phone (888) 744-8119, and description everywhere (entity consistency boosts both SEO and AI corroboration).
