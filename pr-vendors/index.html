Skip the "buy a real WSJ article" idea entirely. If a vendor claims a true WSJ editorial placement for a flat fee, it's either a contributor post, a syndicated press release, or a scam. The contributor/syndication routes above are the honest versions — and far cheaper.

Ask every vendor before paying same 7 checks

  1. Permanent or time-limited? Does the article stay live indefinitely, or get removed after a period?
  2. Do-follow or no-follow links — and how many links / brand mentions are allowed?
  3. Will it index & rank for a "LendPeak" search? Live published URL as proof?
  4. Real editorial URL on the main domain, or a press-release / sponsored sub-section (or a syndicated copy)?
  5. Who writes it — vendor or us? Word count? Can we supply logo / images + contact info?
  6. Labelled "paid / sponsored / press release"? (affects trust & ranking)
  7. Turnaround, refund policy, and is the named outlet truly guaranteed (get it in writing)?
