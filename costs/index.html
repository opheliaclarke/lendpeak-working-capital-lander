2 · What to budget for (set high on purpose)

Two columns: where it should settle once optimized, and the conservative number to budget against so a higher real CPC never catches us out.

Average CPC~$30~$48Real CPC low–high; we plan near the high side
Landing-page conversion (click→lead)8%6%High-intent search + our pre-qual page
CPL (cost per lead)~$375~$800CPC ÷ conversion
Lead → funded8%6%Exclusive, real-time, fast call-back
CPA (cost per funded deal)~$4,700~$13,000CPL ÷ funded rate
Plan the budget against the red column. If real costs land between the green and red (most likely), we're ahead of plan — never behind it. Costs trend toward the green as Quality Score builds and the funded-deal data trains the bidding (typically over the first 60–90 days).

3 · The reality check (4% margin)

Margin ≈ 4% of the funded amount, so the deal size has to carry the CPA:

Funded amountMargin (4%)vs Expected CPA ~$4,700vs Conservative CPA ~$13,000
$50,000$2,000LossLoss
$100,000$4,000~Break-evenLoss
$250,000$10,000Profit~Break-even
$500,000$20,000Strong profitProfit
This is why the campaign leans into big-ticket keywords ($100K–$1M) and a qualifying form. Average deal size — not lead volume — is what makes Google paid search profitable here. We hold budget tight, prove 1 funded deal, measure the real numbers, then scale.
