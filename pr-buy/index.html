What we're skipping and why

On the agency's sheet but not worth buying: worldfinancialreview.com (DA 68 but only 66 visits/mo), fintechnews.org / fintechherald.com (4–5 visits/mo — dead), and walletmonkey / yourmoneysite / bankleave / how2invest (DA 0–14). Too low DA or near-zero traffic — they won't rank, won't get seen, and don't impress on a brand search.

Ask the agency BEFORE quoting Leo

Answers to #1, #3 and #4 are the deal-makers — a no-follow, "paid-post"-tagged article that de-indexes in 90 days is worthless for brand SERP, no matter the DA.

  1. Permanent or time-limited? Does the article stay live forever, or get pulled after X months?
  2. Do-follow or no-follow links — and how many links / brand mentions are allowed?
  3. Will it index & rank for a "LendPeak" search? Can you show a live published URL as proof?
  4. Real editorial URL on the main domain, or a buried /press-release/ / sponsored sub-section?
  5. Who writes it — you or us? Word count? Can we supply logo / images + contact info?
  6. Tagged "paid / sponsored post"? (hurts trust + ranking if so)
  7. Turnaround time + bulk discount for 5–8 together — and refund if a placement gets rejected?
